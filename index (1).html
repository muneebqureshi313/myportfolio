§01MANIFESTO
Research philosophy

How I treat data, bias, and null results.

Reproducibility before elegance. A result only counts once it survives repetition — quantum circuit fidelity gets validated across dozens of simulation runs, and ML models get cross-validated, not just tuned to a single split.

Report the ceiling and the floor. Accuracy numbers are meaningless without the failure modes next to them. Every model I ship comes with its limitations stated as plainly as its headline metric.

Null results are still data. A cipher that resists a quantum attack, or a feature that doesn't improve R², is information worth keeping — not a run to quietly discard.

Method before tool. Qiskit, PyTorch, and scikit-learn are interchangeable; the rationale for choosing a variational ansatz over a brute-force oracle, or a stacking ensemble over a single regressor, is not.

Grover's Search Attack on SPN Block Ciphers

Recovered secret keys for S-AES / Mini-AES with a 100% success rate across 20+ trials.

+
Abstract

Quantum oracle-based key recovery against toy Substitution-Permutation Network ciphers, achieving the theoretical O(√N) quadratic speed-up over classical brute force.

Objective

Test whether Grover's algorithm can practically recover secret keys from S-AES and Mini-AES given a single known plaintext-ciphertext pair.

Method

Built custom quantum oracles and diffuser circuits in Qiskit that mark the correct key state, run inside Jupyter Notebook on the Qiskit Aer simulator.

Data / setup

Known plaintext 6f6d; target key space searched exhaustively via amplitude amplification, repeated over 20+ independent runs.

Synthesis

Measurement histograms of the output register were inspected for a dominant peak corresponding to the marked (correct) key state.

Key insight

Oracle construction, not qubit count, was the binding constraint — a cleanly factored oracle converged reliably where a naive one did not.

Impact

Recovered keys c73d and c45f with 100% success across all 20+ trials, demonstrating quadratic cryptanalytic speed-up in simulation.

Retrospective

Simulator-only: circuit depth and noise on real hardware would erode the speed-up; next step is testing on IBM Quantum hardware with error mitigation.

Variational Quantum Cryptanalysis of 8-bit SPN Ciphers

Hardware-efficient ansatz converged on target keys within 150 optimization iterations.

+
Abstract

A gradient-free variational attack that maps the key-space distribution of an 8-bit toy cipher without requiring a full Grover-style oracle.

Objective

Determine whether a hardware-efficient variational ansatz can isolate a cryptographic key faster than brute-force classical search on constrained hardware.

Method

Designed a problem-specific ansatz in Qiskit; optimized parameters with the gradient-free Nelder–Mead algorithm against classical S-AES/Mini-AES evaluation functions.

Data / setup

Known plaintext 544a; optimizer tracked convergence toward the target key across iterative parameter updates.

Synthesis

Cost-landscape convergence plots were used to confirm the ansatz was narrowing toward a single dominant key hypothesis rather than plateauing.

Key insight

Gradient-free optimization avoided barren-plateau issues that a gradient-based optimizer hit on the same ansatz depth.

Impact

Recovered target key df64, converging within 150 optimization iterations — well inside the resource budget of near-term devices.

Retrospective

Result is cipher-size specific; scaling the ansatz to larger keyspaces without re-triggering barren plateaus remains open.

Hybrid Quantum-Classical CNN for Multi-Disease Detection

National finalist, Pakistan's 1st Quantum Computing Hackathon — 75% accuracy across 4 disease categories.

+
Abstract

A hybrid prototype (team "Qubitects") that feeds classical CNN and audio features into a quantum-encoded classifier for low-resource multi-disease screening.

Objective

Explore whether quantum encoding of classical feature extractors (VGG16, Wav2Vec) improves screening for lung, heart, skin, and breast disease on limited hardware.

Method

Extracted features classically, encoded them via ZZFeatureMap and amplitude encoding, then trained the hybrid network end-to-end using PyTorch's TorchConnector inside Qiskit Machine Learning.

Data / setup

Multi-modal clinical inputs across four disease categories, deployed on a local server for functional testing.

Synthesis

Compared hybrid-model accuracy against the classical-only baseline per disease category to isolate where quantum encoding helped versus added noise.

Key insight

Transferring pretrained classical weights into the quantum encoding stage stabilized training more than initializing the hybrid model from scratch.

Impact

Achieved 75% classification accuracy across 4 disease categories; selected as a national finalist by NCP and CERN's Open Quantum Institute.

Retrospective

75% trails classical-only baselines on some categories — the honest conclusion is quantum encoding is not yet a clear win here, and that gap is the next experiment.

Ensemble ML for Glass-Forming Ability of Bulk Metallic Glasses

Final Year Thesis — R² = 0.93, outperforming baseline Random Forest by 12%.

+
Abstract

A stacking-ensemble regression framework predicting glass-forming ability (GFA) of bulk metallic glass alloys from composition data.

Objective

Predict GFA accurately enough to prioritize which alloy compositions are worth synthesizing experimentally.

Method

Recursive Feature Elimination reduced 12 candidate features to 6; Bayesian optimization tuned hyperparameters; a stacking regressor combined base learners into a meta-model.

Data / setup

5,000+ alloy compositions drawn from simulated and literature-sourced datasets.

Synthesis

Compared stacked-ensemble error (RMSE, MAE) against a Random Forest baseline on held-out compositions to quantify the ensemble's marginal value.

Key insight

Reducing to 6 features via RFE improved generalization rather than hurting it — the extra 6 were mostly adding noise, not signal.

Impact

R² = 0.93, RMSE = 1.42, MAE = 0.77 — a 12% improvement over the Random Forest baseline.

Retrospective

Literature-sourced data carries measurement inconsistencies across labs; a curated, single-protocol dataset is the natural next step.
